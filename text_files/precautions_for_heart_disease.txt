Precautions for Non-Diabetic Individuals to Prevent Heart Disease
---

1. **Maintain a Healthy Diet**:
   - Eat a balanced diet rich in fruits, vegetables, whole grains, and lean proteins.
   - Limit saturated fats, trans fats, and cholesterol.
   - Reduce salt intake to help control blood pressure.

2. **Regular Exercise**:
   - Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous activity each week.
   - Include strength training exercises at least two days a week.

3. **Monitor Blood Pressure**:
   - Regularly check your blood pressure and maintain it within the normal range (less than 120/80 mmHg).

4. **Manage Stress**:
   - Practice stress-reducing techniques such as meditation, yoga, or deep breathing exercises.

5. **Avoid Smoking**:
   - Quit smoking and avoid exposure to secondhand smoke.

6. **Limit Alcohol Consumption**:
   - If you drink alcohol, do so in moderation (up to one drink per day for women and two drinks per day for men).

7. **Maintain a Healthy Weight**:
   - Strive for a healthy weight through diet and exercise. Use BMI as a guideline to determine a healthy weight range.

8. **Regular Health Check-ups**:
   - Visit your healthcare provider regularly for check-ups and screenings, including cholesterol and blood pressure checks.

9. **Stay Hydrated**:
   - Drink plenty of water throughout the day to stay hydrated and support overall health.

10. **Educate Yourself**:
   - Stay informed about heart health and the risk factors associated with heart disease. Knowledge can empower you to make healthier choices.
